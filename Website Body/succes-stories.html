Meet Emmah Mwangi: Championing Digital Financial Literacy

Expert Insight

"Financial Literacy Not Only Enables People to Make Money, But Also Protects Them from Scams"

Emmah Wamboi, a digital financial literacy expert and founder of Digi-Fin Literacy Africa, emphasizes that financial literacy is a critical shield against the rising tide of online fraud targeting unsuspecting Kenyans.

In recent years, online fraudsters have increasingly targeted Kenyans by luring them with seemingly lucrative investment opportunities. Emmah warns that financially illiterate individuals are more vulnerable to such scams due to a lack of basic knowledge about finances and investments.

"You need digital literacy skills to protect your money. Without them, you're likely to fall victim to scammers. Many people have already been scammed online."

Emmah Mwangi, Digital Financial Literacy Expert

Empowering Youth Through Digital Skills in the Gig Economy

With the rise of the gig economy and online income opportunities, Emmah argues that financial literacy is more indispensable than ever. Thanks to the internet, learning finance has become much more accessible.

"Young people should learn how to monetize their skills online, but more importantly, they must learn how to manage that money. Apps and online platforms have made all this possible," she explains.

Our Initiative

What is Skills Share Saturday?

Skills Share Saturday is a community-driven initiative where we bring practical digital financial literacy skills directly to communities across Kenya. Every Saturday, we create safe spaces for learning, sharing, and growing together.

Digital Skills Training

Hands-on sessions teaching safe online transactions, mobile money security, and digital financial management.

Scam Prevention

Learn to identify and avoid online fraud, pyramid schemes, and investment scams targeting vulnerable individuals.

Community Building

Creating trusted networks where community members can share experiences, ask questions, and support each other.
